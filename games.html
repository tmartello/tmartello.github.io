NEW RELEASES

Star Wars The Last Jedi: review

Old legends are laid to rest with all the grace of a drunk ewok. Every character is blatantly written to serve a greater corporate purpose – to hit ‘reset’ on the Star Wars universe and open it up to endless expansion. Star Wars inevitably had to outgrow its origins, but the characters and spirit of the original trilogy (and even the prequels, for all their faults) deserved a softer touch than this heavy-handed, reactionary reset that tries too hard to ‘modernise’ the themes while sacrificing all the magic of the series. They replaced the Force with forcefulness.
